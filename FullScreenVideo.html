Satelites

In the context of spaceflight, a satellite is an object that has been intentionally placed into orbit.
These objects are called artificial satellites to distinguish them from natural satellites such as Earth's Moon.
On 4 October 1957 the Soviet Union launched the world's first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1.
Satellites are used for many purposes. Among several other applications,
they can be used to make star maps and maps of planetary surfaces, and also take pictures of planets they are launched into.
